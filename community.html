Directions

114A Miramar Dr
Mexico Beach, FL 32456
  • Go east on Hwy 98 to Mexico Beach town limits
  • Turn on 42nd street and then turn to your first right on Miramar
  • Continue past the blue and white town homes on your right until you see 114A Miramar

A variety of fish to be caught in Mexico Beach. Charter a boat with a professional captain to guide you to a great spot.

FISHING

If you want to fish, there are options and costs that go along with it. They are all listed on the Mexico Beach website www.mexicobeach.com.

Miss MaryCapt. Chip Blackburn. A good guy and an acquaintance. People like him. Stay's close in but brings in fish. http://www.charterboat-missmary.com/
Charisma Charters Capt. Chuck Gilford (retired military, ex-mayor of MB, businessman). Ok, the big bucks, but big Boats too. Good guy, actually probably the best. He'll run to the deep water. But you'll bring in fish. He has two boats, one run by his son-in-law Danny. He's good too. Trained by Chuck I suspect. One of the things we enjoy doing is walking down to the docks - they tie up/dock just down the street by the boat ramp when they come in to look at the catch . People, companies (they take their key employees on an all expense paid trip) etc. plan their year around a trip like that. http://www.charismacharters.com/

There are plenty of good places to eat in and around Mexico Beach. Check out some of my recommendations.

RESTAURANTS

Fish House - Good food. Actually almost too much. Typically, wife and I would split a dinner and order an extra salad. Full Bar etc. Very good breakfast on the weekends during the season.
http://www.fishhousemexicobeach.com/
Sharon's Cafe - The only Breakfast deal (day to day) in town. It's ok, nothing to write home about. Their Hamburger Platter for lunch is good. No credit cards.
Toucans - Just past Gulf Foods, across the street from the Exxon. Great beach-front view. Fresh Seafood, bar, family friendly.
toucansmexicobeachfl.com
Sunset Grill - in Pt. St. Joe: We have eaten there and it's good. A bit pricey. More gourmet type stuff.
http://www.sunsetcoastalgrill.com/
Peppers - Reid St. in Pt. St. Joe: Absolutely great Mexican Food. An entre easily feeds two.
http://www.peppersmexicangrillandcantina.com/
Killer Seafood - Good, very good. Next to Gulf Foods. Small, owners run everything. No credit cards, erratic hours. Fish Taco delicious. Probably these days, my favorite eat out place in MB.
http://www.killerseafood.net/
Mango Marley's - Next to Fish House in MB. 25 different wing sauces. Good drinks, burgers, seafood. Another Family friendly place.
http://mangomarleys.com/

Some other information you might find useful during your stay.

MY OTHER TIPS

Groceries and General Supplies - Gulf Foods in MB, good meat. The normal stuff you might need. Real Grocery store in Port St. Joe, the Piggly Wiggly.
The beach across the canal - You can walk for miles. No development yet, and it's nice. You'll see where they've marked the turtle nests certain times of year.
The Marina at Pt. St. Joe - Interesting to walk the docks. Don't let anyone tell you there isn't money in this country. Some people like the restaurant there.
Apalachicola - 30 mi. east of MB on hwy 98. Very interesting old town. Home of the oysters. Some very interesting shops, old homes, and antique type places. If you go, be sure to visit the old tin barn antique place down next to the river. Nice walk around town. Some interesting restaurants too. They have the Seafood Festival every year during the first weekend of Nov. It's a hoot, especially the Oyster Eating Contest. Across the big bridge you come to Eastpoint. You can buy good fresh seafood and oysters there. The good stuff.
Cape San Blas - Voted one of the top 10 beaches in the world. Half of it is a state park. Take C30A out of Pt. St. Joe and follow the signs. Been there, done that, it doesn't appeal that much to me. To some people it does. It is a pretty beach though. The old Cape San Blas lighthouse is very interesting. The Cape San Blas lighthouse has now been moved to Port St. Joe.
If you smoke, buy your cigarettes in GA. Before you go. It's about $10.00 a carton higher in FL
Buy automobile gas at the AL/FL line. It's typically .20 cents higher a gallon in FL. Gas these days is cheaper in AL than GA.
If you like a couple of drinks in the evening, buy enough in GA, it's a lot cheaper. Beer is about the same.
The nearest WalMart is in Callaway, back towards PC in case you forget anything. If you have to go to WalMart, then go early and stop at Corams (in Parker, just before Callaway) for breakfast. Their hashbrown casarole is to die for. It'll feed two easy.
If you want to cook in w/ seafood, the Shell Shack in MB has fresh stuff and they're not too bad price-wise.
